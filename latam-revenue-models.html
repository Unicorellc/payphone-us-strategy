Colombia Betting Expansion Playbook

Phase 1: API Integration

Offer the API (tokenization + web transaction + payouts) to 2–3 Coljuegos-licensed operators. The API is the most betting-ready product today. Lead with card deposits + Pagos Masivos for payouts.

Phase 2: Add PSE + Wallets

Integrate PSE for bank transfer deposits and Nequi/Daviplata as deposit methods. This captures the 55% of banked Colombians who prefer non-card payments for betting.

Phase 3: Cash Deposits via Efecty

Enable cash-in at 12K+ Efecty locations. This is the key to reaching the 40% cash-dependent population. Generate voucher in betting app → pay at Efecty → funds credited instantly.

Phase 4: Payphone as Betting Wallet

Position the Payphone wallet + Mastercard as the player's betting account. Deposit from any source, winnings go to wallet, spend via card. Becomes a closed-loop ecosystem.

Coljuegos Advantage

Colombia's Coljuegos license is ~$120K — cheapest in LATAM. No IEPS-equivalent tax on gaming revenue (unlike Mexico's 30%). This makes Colombia the highest-margin betting market in the region.

Card Gap → Opportunity

The Payphone Mastercard can't do ATM withdrawals today. For betting, this is actually a feature: winnings stay in the Payphone ecosystem. But add Nequi/Daviplata withdrawal as an alternative cash-out path.

Colombia Strategic Takeaways

🚀 Fast to Market

Lower upfront investment ($570K) vs Mexico ($896K). Cheaper Coljuegos license ($120K) makes betting viable from day one.

💳 Wallet Ecosystem

Nequi + Daviplata = 32M combined users. This is a major differentiator that doesn't exist at this scale in most other LATAM markets.

⚠️ GMF Tax Drag

The 0.4% financial movement tax is unavoidable and eats into margins on every bank transfer. Build this into pricing from day one.

💸 FX Opportunity

COP volatility creates wider FX spreads. The +0.9% FX margin is higher than Mexico's +0.8% due to currency risk premium.

🎯 Betting is Key

At ~3.1% net margin, the betting vertical is 2x more profitable than standard card processing. Coljuegos license should be a Year 1 priority.

📈 Path to Scale

Launch cross-border via dLocal, migrate to Redeban/Credibanco as volume grows. Target 5,000 merchants by Year 3 for $451M TPV.

Mexico Betting Expansion Playbook

Phase 1: API + SPEI

Lead with the API (tokenization + web transaction) for card deposits, and SPEI for instant bank transfers. SPEI is the killer feature — nearly free infrastructure, instant settlement, and highest margin rail for both deposits and payouts.

Phase 2: OXXO Cash Deposits

Integrate OXXO Pay for cash deposits. With 21K+ locations and ~50% cash dependency in Mexico, this is critical. Generate reference codes in betting apps, players pay at OXXO. Accept 2-day settlement delay.

Phase 3: SPEI Payouts at Scale

Use Pagos Masivos for batch SPEI payouts to winners. SPEI costs ~MXN $3/tx but is instant. This is the primary cash-out method — faster and cheaper than card-based payouts. Position this as a key differentiator.

Phase 4: Full Ecosystem

Offer Payphone wallet as a betting super-wallet: deposit via SPEI/OXXO/card, withdraw via SPEI to any bank. The Payphone Mastercard supplements this for online spending of winnings.

IEPS Tax Strategy

The 30% IEPS tax hits gaming revenue hard. Mitigate by: (1) pricing deposits at 5%+ to absorb tax, (2) making margin on SPEI payouts which are tax-free, (3) focusing on volume over margin. MX market is 2.5x larger than CO.

SPEI > Card for Cash-Out

Since the Payphone Mastercard has no ATM withdrawal, use SPEI as the primary payout rail to player bank accounts. SPEI is instant and reaches every bank in Mexico. The card becomes supplementary for international spending.

Mexico Strategic Takeaways

🚀 The Bigger Prize

Mexico's $30B e-commerce market is 2.5x Colombia's. Year 3 EBITDA of $8.2M is 4x Colombia's $2.0M. Higher investment, but massively higher returns.

⚡ SPEI is a Superpower

SPEI costs ~$0.15/tx but you charge ~$0.60/tx. As SPEI grows to 25% of mix by Year 3, it becomes the highest-margin rail in the entire model. No equivalent exists in Colombia.

⚠️ IEPS Tax on Betting

The 30% IEPS tax on gaming revenue significantly reduces betting margins vs Colombia. Consider whether the $500K+ SEGOB license is worth it given the tax drag.

📜 Ley Fintech Compliance

CNBV registration under Ley Fintech adds $10K–$30K and ongoing compliance overhead. Budget for dedicated legal counsel in Mexico City.

🏢 OXXO is Critical

With ~50% cash dependency, OXXO Pay is essential for reaching the unbanked. 21K+ locations make it the largest cash-in network. Plan for 2-day settlement delays.

📈 Margin Improvement

Gross margin improves from 11% (Y1) to 37.2% (Y3) as you shift from cross-border to local acquirers and SPEI grows. The economics get dramatically better at scale.

Combined LATAM Strategy

🇨🇴 Launch Colombia First

Lower investment ($570K vs $896K), faster breakeven (~18 mo vs ~22 mo), no gaming tax. Use Colombia as the beachhead to prove the model before scaling to Mexico.

🇲🇽 Scale in Mexico

2.5x bigger market, 4x higher Year 3 EBITDA ($8.2M vs $2.0M), SPEI is a superpower. Mexico is the bigger prize but needs more capital and longer runway.

📦 Product Suite Ready

API + Pagos Masivos are betting-ready today. No new product development needed for Phase 1 in either market. Lead with API integration for deposits and batch payouts.

💳 Card Gap Strategy

No ATM/ACH on Payphone Mastercard. In Colombia, use Nequi/Daviplata for payouts. In Mexico, use SPEI. The card becomes a spending tool, not a cash-out tool.

📈 Combined Y3 Opportunity

$1.4B TPV, $10.2M EBITDA, $42.2M combined revenue across both markets. Total investment ~$1.5M with full payback by end of Year 2.

🎲 Betting is the Margin Play

Betting delivers 2–3x higher margin than standard card processing in both markets. Prioritize Coljuegos license (CO) in Year 1, SEGOB (MX) in Year 2 once volume proves the model.
