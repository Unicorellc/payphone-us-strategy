Remittances

Licensed Money Transmitter Sponsor Model

Faster time-to-market by operating under an existing MTL holder's licenses.

üîÑ How Sponsor Model Works

  1. Licensed MTL holds state licenses in all target states
  2. Payphone becomes authorized agent under sponsor
  3. Transactions flow through sponsor's licensed entity
  4. Sponsor handles regulatory compliance & reporting
  5. Payphone focuses on customer experience & growth

‚úÖ Benefits & Requirements

  • Faster time-to-market (weeks vs. months)
  • Lower capital requirements
  • Reduced compliance burden
  • Immediate access to all 50 states
What Payphone Still Needs:
  • Agent Registration (sponsor typically handles)
  • Own AML/BSA Compliance Program
  • Written policies, compliance officer, training

‚ö†Ô∏è Due Diligence & Economics

Verify Sponsor Licenses:

Confirm sponsor holds active licenses in ALL target states: TX, CA, FL, AZ, NJ, IL. Check NMLS Consumer Access.

Revenue Share:

Sponsors typically take 15-30% of transaction fees. Factor into unit economics and pricing strategy.

Alternative Path

Earned Wage Access (EWA) Alternative

Many states classify EWA differently than payday loans, offering a simpler regulatory path.

üí° What is Earned Wage Access?

EWA allows workers to access earned (but unpaid) wages before payday. Unlike payday loans, it's tied to hours already worked. Many states classify EWA differently, offering a simpler regulatory path.

If Payphone structures as true EWA:
  • ‚úì Simplify AZ compliance (not a loan)
  • ‚úì Faster CA path (EWA registration vs. CFL)
  • ‚úì Less capital required
  • ‚úì Better regulatory optics

Trade-off: Requires employer/payroll integration

‚úì EWA-Friendly States (Not Loans)

  • ‚úì Nevada - First EWA licensing (July 2024)
  • ‚úì Missouri - Registration only
  • ‚úì Kansas - Registration required (July 2024)
  • ‚úì Wisconsin - Licensed but not loan
  • ‚úì South Carolina - Registration
  • ‚úì Utah - Effective May 2025
  • ‚úì Indiana - Effective Jan 2026

‚ö†Ô∏è States Treating EWA as Loans

  • ‚ö†Ô∏è California - EWA = loans, but exempt if registered by Feb 2025
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è Connecticut - Subject to usury caps
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è Maryland - New law treats EWA as loans
